Japan’s proposed “National Intelligence Bureau” under the Takaichi administration is drawing attention as a mechanism to centralize intelligence and speed up security decisions. The move comes amid rising economic-security pressures around submarine cables, defense supply chains, and revisions to national security documents. The US and UK already offer precedents for how intelligence is coordinated at the center of government, making organizational design the key issue for Japan.
